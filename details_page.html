The model:
The IS-LM model is a macroeconomic tool that illustrates the correlation between interest rates and real output.
It establishes a point of intersection between the "investment-saving" (IS) and "liquidity preference-money supply" (LM) curves,
which signifies a state of general equilibrium where simultaneous equilibria are assumed to exist in both the goods and asset markets.
John Hicks initially developed this model in 1937, and it was subsequently expanded upon by Alvin Hansen.
It serves as a mathematical representation of Keynesian macroeconomic theory.
From the 1940s to the mid-1970s, it held a dominant position as the primary framework for macroeconomic analysis.
